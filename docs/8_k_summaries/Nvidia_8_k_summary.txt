NVIDIA Corporation reported a record-breaking financial performance for the fourth quarter and fiscal year ended January 28, 2024. The company announced a quarterly revenue of $22.1 billion, marking a 22% increase from the previous quarter and a staggering 265% increase from the previous year. The data center revenue was a significant contributor, reaching $18.4 billion for the quarter, up 27% from the previous quarter and up 409% from the previous year. For the fiscal year 2024, NVIDIA's revenue was $60.9 billion, up 126% from the previous year, with GAAP earnings per diluted share at $11.93, up 586% from the previous year. The company attributed its success to the surging demand for accelerated computing and generative AI across various industries and nations. NVIDIA's CEO, Jensen Huang, highlighted the company's diverse drivers of growth, including demand for data processing, training, and inference from large cloud-service providers, as well as enterprise software and consumer internet companies. The company also announced a quarterly cash dividend of $0.04 per share and provided an outlook for the first quarter of fiscal 2025, expecting revenue to be around $24.0 billion.
