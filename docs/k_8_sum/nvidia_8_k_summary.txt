NVIDIA Corporation reported a record-breaking financial performance for the fourth quarter and fiscal year 2024, with significant year-over-year growth. The company's Q4 revenue reached $22.1 billion, a 265% increase from the previous year, and fiscal year revenue soared to $60.9 billion, marking a 126% rise. This growth was driven by substantial demand for NVIDIA's Data Center platform, particularly for applications in accelerated computing and generative AI. The company's Data Center revenue alone hit a record $18.4 billion in Q4, up 409% from the previous year. NVIDIA also reported record quarterly and full-year revenues in other segments, including Gaming and Professional Visualization. The company's GAAP earnings per diluted share for Q4 were $4.93, up 765% year-over-year, with non-GAAP earnings per diluted share at $5.16. For fiscal 2024, GAAP earnings per diluted share were $11.93, a 586% increase, and non-GAAP earnings per diluted share were $12.96. NVIDIA anticipates revenue of $24.0 billion for Q1 fiscal 2025, with GAAP and non-GAAP gross margins expected to be around 76.3% and 77.0%, respectively.
